# CardLift Alpha Testing Guide

First, thank you.

By participating in this private Alpha, you're helping shape CardLift
before a broader public release.

Our goal isn't simply to extract cards---it's to make working with
print-and-play PDFs feel simple and trustworthy.

This Alpha is already capable of handling real projects, but there are
definitely areas that can still be improved. Your feedback will help
shape those improvements.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# How to Get the Most from This Alpha

We recommend trying two workflows.

## 1. Start with the Demo Deck

The built-in Demo Deck introduces the complete CardLift workflow in
just a few minutes.

It provides a quick way to become familiar with the application before
importing one of your own print-and-play PDFs.

## 2. Try One of Your Own PDFs

After completing the Demo Deck, try CardLift with a real project that
matters to you.

The most valuable feedback often comes from using the software on an
actual game you intend to play.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# We'd Especially Like Your Feedback On

Rather than trying to find every small bug, we're most interested in
understanding your experience.

For example:

-   Was the overall workflow easy to understand?
-   Did the Demo Deck prepare you for using your own PDF?
-   Was calibration intuitive?
-   Did the review screen help you catch mistakes before exporting?
-   Were the exported cards what you expected?
-   Was anything confusing or surprising?
-   If something didn't work as expected, was it clear what happened?

If you ever find yourself wondering,

> "What am I supposed to do next?"

...we would especially like to hear about that.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Reporting Feedback

Please share feedback in the **#cardlift-alpha** channel on the **RPG
Frequencies Discord** server.

Helpful information includes:

-   the PDF you were using (if it can be shared)
-   what you expected to happen
-   what actually happened
-   screenshots or exported images (when helpful)

Not every report needs to be a bug.

Suggestions, usability observations, and first impressions are just as
valuable.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# About This Alpha

This is an Alpha release.

You may encounter bugs, rough edges, or workflows that can still be
improved.

At this stage we're focused on improving reliability, usability, and the
overall experience before a broader Beta release.
